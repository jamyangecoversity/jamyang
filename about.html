About Jamyang Ecoversity

An emerging living-learning center in Bhutan, reconnecting education with nature, culture, and purpose.

Where We Come From

Jamyang Ecoversity grew from a simple question: what would education look like if it taught us how to live well — not just how to earn a living? In a time of ecological crisis and growing disconnection, we saw the need for a different kind of learning. One rooted in land, shaped by tradition, and oriented toward regeneration.

We are now building that vision in Bhutan — creating a place where hands-on practice, cultural wisdom, ecological care, and inner growth come together in a single, living educational experience.

What We Believe

Education Should Be Lived

The deepest learning happens through doing — in the garden, the workshop, and the community, not only in the classroom.

Wisdom Lives in Place

Real knowledge is connected to land, culture, and community. When we learn from where we are, we learn who we are.

Success Is More Than a Career

A good education prepares people for meaningful lives — not just jobs, but purpose, health, relationships, and contribution.

Traditional Knowledge Matters

Ancestral practices and indigenous wisdom hold vital answers for the challenges we face today and tomorrow.

What We Are Building

Jamyang Ecoversity is developing a hands-on, place-based model of education where learners grow food, build with natural materials, practice traditional crafts, and develop the inner clarity to live with purpose.

Our approach is designed to be rooted in Bhutan but adaptable anywhere. We weave together ecological practice, cultural knowledge, practical skill-building, and personal development into an integrated learning experience.

Our Vision

A world where education nurtures grounded, capable, and community-centered people who lead the transition to regenerative ways of living.

Our Mission

To create a living-learning space where people develop the skills, values, and purpose to live meaningfully and contribute to a thriving future for all life.

What Guides Us

Regeneration

We heal and restore ecosystems, communities, and relationships through everything we learn and practice.

Rootedness

We connect deeply to place, culture, and community while remaining open to wisdom from all traditions.

Learning by Doing

We ground all knowledge in hands-on experience — in the field, the workshop, and the kitchen.

Community

We learn together, support one another, and take collective responsibility for our shared future.

Stewardship

We care for the land, water, air, and all living systems that sustain life on Earth.

Wholeness

Meet the Founder

The founder of Jamyang Ecoversity is a nature-rooted visionary dedicated to reimagining education as a pathway to a more meaningful, balanced, and regenerative life.

From childhood, he felt most alive in nature — walking through forests, hiking mountain trails, watching birds and animals, and finding joy in the living world. He was also deeply drawn to music, singing, drama, and creative expression. Though he learned best through doing, observing, and direct experience, he followed the formal education system believing it was the path to dignity, freedom, and a better future.

Over time, however, he came to see a painful truth: much of modern education does not truly prepare people to live joyful, grounded, and purposeful lives. Instead, it often leads to stress, disconnection, competition, and the endless pursuit of external success. It teaches people how to fit into systems, but not always how to live wisely, meaningfully, and in harmony with life.

This realization became a turning point.

He is now returning to Bhutan to help co-create an alternative model of education — one rooted in nature, culture, practical wisdom, regenerative living, inner growth, and community wellbeing. Through personal experiment and lived example, he hopes to serve as a catalyst for change and inspire younger generations to reconnect with themselves, with the land, and with a deeper purpose in life.

Jamyang Ecoversity is born from that vision: a heartfelt offering for future generations, and a step toward creating a better life for children, grandchildren, and the earth we all share.
