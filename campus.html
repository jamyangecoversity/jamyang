Campus & Model

A living-learning environment being designed and built as a demonstration site for regenerative education in Bhutan.

The Campus as Living Teacher

We are designing a campus that is more than a physical space — it will be a living laboratory where regenerative principles are practiced daily. Every building, garden, and system is planned to serve as both a functional facility and an educational tool, demonstrating how ecological design, traditional wisdom, and appropriate technology can create resilient human habitats.

When complete, learning will happen through direct experience with working systems. Learners will not just study regenerative practices — they will live them, participating in systems that heal soil, harvest water, build community, and cultivate wellbeing.

Planned Campus Infrastructure

Each element of the campus is being designed to demonstrate regenerative principles and serve multiple educational and functional purposes.

Learning Hall & Classrooms

Naturally lit spaces built with local materials, designed for flexible learning configurations and community gatherings.

Library & Resource Center

Collection of books, digital resources, and traditional knowledge archives on regenerative practices and ecological wisdom.

Administration Area

Efficient workspace demonstrating sustainable office practices and community organizational models.

Demonstration Farm Plots

Working examples of regenerative agriculture, agroforestry, and food production systems.

Composting & Nursery Area

Soil fertility center with various composting methods and plant propagation facilities.

Food Forest & Medicinal Garden

Perennial food systems and healing plant gardens demonstrating ecological design principles.

Workshops for Building & Crafts

Hands-on learning spaces for natural building, traditional crafts, and appropriate technology.

Learner Accommodation

Simple, comfortable housing demonstrating natural building techniques and sustainable living.

Kitchen & Dining Area

Community food preparation space featuring local, organic ingredients and traditional cooking methods.

Sanitation & Water Systems

Ecological sanitation, greywater recycling, and rainwater harvesting demonstration systems.

Wellness & Contemplative Spaces

Quiet areas for meditation, reflection, movement practices, and community care activities.

Storage & Production Space

Facilities for food processing, tool storage, and small-scale regenerative enterprise activities.

Community Gathering Area

Central space for events, celebrations, markets, and community-building activities.

Designed for Learning, Built for Living

Every structure on campus is both a functional facility and a living demonstration of regenerative principles in action.
