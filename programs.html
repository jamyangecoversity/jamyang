Learning & Programs

Hands-on learning areas and emerging programs for regenerative living, rooted in Bhutan and designed for the world.

Learning by Doing, Living by Learning

Our curriculum is built around interconnected learning areas — not isolated subjects. Learners engage with the land, with traditional knowledge holders, and with each other through direct practice, reflection, and real-world projects. We are developing these programs now, with the first offerings emerging as the campus takes shape.

Core Learning Areas

These four domains form the foundation of our first programs and are actively being developed.

01

Regenerative Agriculture and Food Systems

Learn to cultivate food in ways that heal ecosystems, build soil health, and create resilient local food systems.

Soil Health & Composting Seed Saving & Biodiversity Agroecology Permaculture Design Water Stewardship Kitchen Gardening Climate-Resilient Farming
02

Natural Building and Ecological Design

Create beautiful, functional structures using local, natural materials and ecological principles.

Vernacular Architecture Earth-Based Construction Bamboo & Timber Systems Natural Plasters & Finishes Rainwater Harvesting Site Planning
03

Traditional Knowledge and Cultural Literacy

Connect with ancestral wisdom and cultural practices that sustain communities and ecological relationships.

Indigenous Ecological Knowledge Crafts & Artisanal Skills Intergenerational Learning Oral Traditions Land-Based Practices
04

Holistic Wellbeing

Cultivate physical, mental, emotional, and spiritual health through integrated practices and community care.

Mindfulness & Presence Physical Health & Movement Nutrition & Food Wisdom Community Care Responsible Living

Learning That Reconnects Head, Heart, and Hands

Every domain is experienced through direct practice, mentorship, and real-world application — never in isolation.

Emerging Learning Areas

As Jamyang Ecoversity grows, these additional domains will expand our curriculum and deepen the learning experience.

05

Regenerative Livelihoods and Social Enterprise

Create meaningful work that serves people and planet while providing dignified livelihoods.

Livelihood Design Ethical Entrepreneurship Cooperative Models Financial Literacy

Leadership, Purpose, and Community Engagement

Values-Based Leadership Communication & Facilitation Community Organizing Service Learning
Now Developing

Foundational Learning Program

An immersive experience in regenerative living — our first flagship offering

Duration

3–6 months immersive learning

Format

Residential, hands-on, community-based

Who It's For

Youth and adults seeking purposeful, practical education

Status

Currently being designed — first cohort opening soon

What You'll Learn

This program will provide integrated training in regenerative agriculture, natural building, traditional knowledge, and holistic wellbeing. Through daily practice, mentorship, and project-based learning, participants will develop the skills and clarity to create meaningful work and contribute to their communities.

Key Outcomes

  • Practical skills in regenerative agriculture and natural building
  • Connection with traditional knowledge and cultural wisdom
  • Personal wellbeing and inner development practices
  • Confidence and clarity for meaningful livelihood
