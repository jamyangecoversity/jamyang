Our Impact

Tracking the transformation we create in people, communities, and living systems around the world.

Creating Regenerative Change

We measure success not just by numbers, but by the depth of transformation in people's lives, the health of the ecosystems we touch, and the resilience of communities we serve. Our impact framework captures both tangible outputs and the deeper outcomes that reflect our mission of nurturing regenerative futures — locally and globally.

Expected Outputs

The concrete deliverables and tangible results we commit to producing through our programs and operations.

Pilot Site Established

Functional demonstration campus showcasing regenerative systems in practice.

Foundational Curriculum Developed

Comprehensive educational framework integrating eight learning areas.

Demonstration Systems Operational

Working examples of regenerative agriculture, natural building, and ecological design.

First Learner Cohort Trained

Graduates equipped with practical skills and regenerative mindset.

Workshops Delivered

Community-based training programs reaching diverse audiences.

Partnerships Formed

Strategic collaborations with communities, organizations, and institutions.

Educational Model Documented

Comprehensive resources for replication and adaptation in other contexts.

Public Awareness Strengthened

Increased understanding of regenerative education and its importance.

Short- to Medium-Term Outcomes

The changes and benefits we expect to see within 1-3 years of program implementation.

Improved Practical Skills

Learners demonstrate competence in regenerative agriculture, natural building, traditional crafts, and ecological stewardship. They can apply these skills to create tangible improvements in their homes and communities.

Stronger Connection to Land & Culture

Participants develop deeper relationships with local ecosystems, traditional knowledge, and cultural practices. They report increased sense of belonging and identity rooted in place.

Increased Dignity Around Local Livelihoods

Community members regain pride in traditional occupations and practical knowledge. Young people see meaningful career pathways in regenerative work.

Awareness of Alternative Education Models

Educators, parents, and policymakers recognize the value of hands-on, place-based learning. Demand grows for similar programs in other communities.

Livelihood Pathways Created

Graduates launch regenerative enterprises, find meaningful employment, or create community projects. Local economies benefit from new sustainable businesses.

Community Resilience Enhanced

Communities demonstrate greater capacity to adapt to challenges, solve problems collectively, and maintain wellbeing during transitions.

Long-Term Outcomes

The transformative changes we aim to contribute to over 5-10 years and beyond.

Viable Model of Regenerative Education

Our educational approach proves replicable and scalable, inspiring similar institutions globally. The model influences mainstream education policy and practice.

Stronger Ecological & Community Resilience

Communities where we work demonstrate measurable improvements in ecosystem health, food security, water quality, and social cohesion.

Revitalization of Practical Wisdom

Traditional knowledge and practical skills are valued, preserved, and adapted for contemporary relevance. Intergenerational knowledge transfer flourishes.

Youth Empowerment & Meaningful Work

Young people have clear pathways to purposeful work that serves their communities and the planet. Youth unemployment and brain drain decrease.

Cultural Shift in Education & Success

Society recognizes diverse forms of intelligence and success. Education is valued for its role in creating whole human beings, not just workers.

From Intention to Transformation

Every program, partnership, and practice is designed to ripple outward — creating lasting change far beyond our campus.

Monitoring & Evaluation

Our approach to measuring progress, learning from experience, and ensuring accountability to our mission and stakeholders.

Key Indicators

Learning & Skills

  • Number of learners enrolled and completed
  • Skills competency assessments
  • Portfolio development and project completion
  • Post-program employment/enterprise creation

Ecological Impact

  • Land under regenerative management
  • Soil health improvements
  • Biodiversity increases
  • Water conservation and quality
  • Carbon sequestration

Community Benefits

  • Workshops delivered and participants reached
  • Partnerships formed and active
  • Community participation rates
  • Local economic benefits

Personal Transformation

  • Participant stories and testimonials
  • Wellbeing and resilience assessments
  • Values and mindset shifts
  • Leadership development

Evaluation Methods

Participatory Evaluation

Learners, community members, and partners actively participate in assessing program effectiveness and identifying improvements.

Story-Based Assessment

Qualitative approaches that capture personal transformation, community impact, and unexpected outcomes through narrative.

Ecological Monitoring

Regular assessment of ecosystem health indicators on campus and in community projects to measure environmental impact.

Economic Analysis

Tracking livelihood creation, enterprise development, and local economic benefits to demonstrate financial sustainability.

Longitudinal Studies

Following graduates and community impacts over time to understand lasting effects and identify areas for improvement.

Peer Review

External evaluation by experts in regenerative education, ecological design, and community development.

Learning & Adaptation

We view monitoring not as judgment, but as learning. Our evaluation processes are designed to:

  • Inform program improvement: Using data and feedback to continuously refine our approach
  • Demonstrate accountability: Ensuring we deliver on our promises to stakeholders
  • Share learning: Contributing to the broader field of regenerative education
  • Celebrate success: Recognizing achievements and motivating continued effort
  • Identify challenges: Honestly addressing difficulties and adapting strategies
